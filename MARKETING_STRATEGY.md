# MeetingPrep AI - Marketing Strategy

## üéØ LinkedIn Content Marketing

### **Challenge: Brand Separation**
- User already has existing business on LinkedIn
- Posting about new product could appear as "jumping between ideas"
- Need targeted approach without confusing existing audience

### **Solution: Targeted LinkedIn Strategies**

#### **1. LinkedIn Newsletter (Recommended)**
- Create separate newsletter: "Meeting Efficiency Weekly"
- Opt-in subscribers only (no spam to main followers)
- Build targeted audience interested in meeting prep
- Professional way to establish expertise

#### **2. LinkedIn Groups Targeting**
- Join sales/meeting-focused groups:
  - Sales Navigator Users
  - Revenue Operations Professionals
  - Business Development Networks
  - Account Executive Communities
- Post in groups instead of main feed
- Targets interested audience without broadcasting to all followers

#### **3. Direct Messaging Campaigns**
- Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Target: Sales Managers, Account Executives, Business Development
- Personalized messages about meeting prep challenges
- Higher conversion rates than public posts

#### **4. LinkedIn Ads (Micro-Budget: ¬£20-50/month)**
- Sponsored content targeting specific job titles
- Only shows to target audience
- Doesn't appear as personal content
- Test messaging before organic rollout

#### **5. Content Segmentation**
- **Main feed:** Continue existing business content
- **Comments:** Engage in meeting/productivity discussions
- **Articles:** Write about meeting efficiency (without mentioning tool initially)
- **Stories:** MeetingPrep content (24hr expiration)

### **Brand Positioning Options**
- **Stealth Launch:** Share as "interesting tool I found"
- **Portfolio Approach:** Position as serial problem-solver
- **Separate Company Page:** MeetingPrep AI company page with own followers

3. **Business Development:** Prospect research automation

### **Outreach Channels**

#### **LinkedIn Direct Messages**
- Personalized connection requests
- Value-first approach (meeting prep tips)
- Soft introduction to tool after relationship building
- Follow-up sequence over 2-3 weeks

#### **Email Outreach**
- Sales team directories
- Industry newsletters
- Professional associations
- Conference attendee lists

#### **Community Engagement**
- Sales Slack communities
- Reddit productivity forums
- Facebook sales groups
- Industry Discord servers

### **Message Templates**

#### **LinkedIn Connection Request:**
```
Hi [Name], I noticed you're in [role] at [company]. I've been researching meeting preparation efficiency in sales - would love to connect and share some insights I've gathered about reducing prep time.
```

#### **Follow-up Message:**
```
Thanks for connecting! I've been working on solutions for the 15-30 minutes most sales professionals spend researching before each meeting. Have you found any good systems for streamlining this process?
```

#### **Value-First Email:**
```
Subject: 5-minute meeting prep hack for [Company] sales team

Hi [Name],

I noticed [Company] has been expanding rapidly. With more client meetings, I imagine your team spends significant time on pre-meeting research.

I've compiled a quick guide on reducing meeting prep from 30 minutes to 5 minutes using automation. Would this be useful for your team?

[Include 2-3 actionable tips]

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

### **Outreach Metrics**
- **Target:** 50 personalized messages/week
- **Response Rate Goal:** 15-20%
- **Conversion to Demo:** 25% of responses
- **Demo to Trial:** 50%
- **Trial to Paid:** 30%

### **Follow-up Sequence**
- **Day 1:** Initial outreach
- **Day 7:** Value-add follow-up (no pitch)
- **Day 14:** Soft tool introduction
- **Day 21:** Direct demo offer
- **Day 30:** Final follow-up with case study
